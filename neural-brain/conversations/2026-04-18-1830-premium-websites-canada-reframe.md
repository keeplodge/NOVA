# Startup Reframe — Premium Small-Business Websites, Canada-Wide

Correction and reframe of the earlier "high-turnaround websites for small businesses in small towns" idea. Sir clarified the correct shape: this is NOT a KeepLodge-style productized volume play on shared templates. It's premium bespoke websites for small businesses across Canada, with a separate style template for each client, delivered fast. Keep this memory load-bearing; the earlier 2026-04-18-1800 note describes a direction Sir has abandoned.

## Correct business shape

- **Premium small-business websites across Canada.** Not just small towns — all of Canada. Urban, semi-urban, rural.
- **Each site gets its own style template.** No shared template engine output. The visual identity per client is bespoke.
- **Price point: premium tier ($3K–$15K range, one-time + potential retainer).** Not the $300-$1K volume play. This is "expensive but worth it" positioning, not "cheap and cheerful."
- **Turnaround is the wedge.** Traditional premium agencies take 6-12 weeks. The play is delivering the same or higher design quality in days-to-two-weeks by letting AI do what used to need a design team and a dev team.
- **Volume target: 5-10 sites/month.** At $5K avg = $25-50K/month. At $10K avg = $50-100K/month. Very different financial profile than the volume play.

## Why this shape works

- **Claude Design (launched 2026-04-17) is the unlock.** It produces unique visual prototypes per client in minutes. Hand-off bundle into Claude Code for production build. This collapses the traditional "design team week + dev team weeks" pipeline.
- **Canada has a premium-deprived small-business web market.** Agencies that deliver good work charge $20K+ and take months. Wix/Squarespace is generic. The gap between those two tiers is where this business sits.
- **Bespoke-per-client defensibility.** Every site is genuinely custom, so this isn't a commodity. Hard to replicate because the competitor would need the same Claude Design + Claude Code tooling AND the taste to direct it.
- **Canadian scope is strategic.** Sir is Toronto-based — local credibility matters for premium sales. Canada-wide (not US-wide) keeps the addressable market focused, legally simpler, and the accent/cultural-fit advantage in play.

## How it differs from KeepLodge (important — do not conflate)

| Dimension | KeepLodge | This startup |
|-|-|-|
| Customer | STR hosts / property managers | Premium small businesses any industry |
| Geography | Global (wherever STRs operate) | Canada-only |
| Template | Shared template engine, same structure per client with client data injected | Unique style + structure per client |
| Delivery | Self-serve-ish, productized | Hands-on premium service |
| Price | SaaS monthly + setup | Project-based, premium one-time + optional retainer |

The template engine that KeepLodge uses is NOT the production stack for this startup. That engine is optimized for "same site shape, different client data." Premium bespoke requires actual design work per client — which is what Claude Design enables.

## Claude Design in the loop

- Claude Design (Anthropic, launched 2026-04-17, web-only, Opus 4.7) produces visual prototypes and mockups from prompts/uploads/document imports.
- Hand-off bundle from Claude Design feeds into Claude Code CLI, which builds the production site.
- Workflow becomes: intake call with client → Claude Design produces mood/direction/mockup → Claude Code builds production code → SEO / schema / local optimization baked in automatically → delivery in 7-14 days.
- Sir's role: client relationship + final polish pass + positioning/pricing/selling. AI does the rest of the production labor.

## Open strategic questions (for the /idea evaluation next)

1. **First industry vertical.** Do all industries at once, or pick one (restaurants? Trades? Professional services?) for the first 10 clients so case studies compound within a niche?
2. **Pricing anchor.** Single tier ($7,500 flat) vs. three tiers (Good/Better/Best at $3K/$7K/$12K) vs. custom-quoted per scope.
3. **Retainer model.** Ongoing $300-$500/mo for hosting + SEO monitoring + monthly edits vs. one-time-only, client manages hosting themselves.
4. **Sales channel first 10 clients.** Cold outreach? Referrals from Sir's existing network? LinkedIn? Specific local Chambers of Commerce? Toronto-first then expand?
5. **Brand positioning.** New independent brand, or sub-brand under Sir's existing identity? Name?
6. **Legal/contract layer.** Canada-specific small-business contract templates, scope-creep protection, payment terms.
7. **What makes a client say yes in 15 minutes on a $7K site?** The Claude Design prototype demo delivered *during the sales call* may be the closer — they see their actual site direction in real time.

## What NOT to do

- Do not build a KeepLodge-style template engine for this. Wrong shape.
- Do not price low. The whole wedge is premium-delivered-fast. Low pricing kills the positioning.
- Do not target "all small businesses everywhere." Pick a vertical + a city for the first 10 clients.
